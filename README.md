##DESCRIPTION##

Sentiment Analysis — Social Media Text Data
This project performs a sentiment analysis on a dataset of 14 real social media posts and news headlines covering topics such as gaming, technology, politics, and sports, using Microsoft Excel to build a complete scoring and classification system without any coding. Each raw text entry was cleaned and converted to lowercase, then scored using a custom keyword-based approach where positive words such as "love", "best", and "thank you" and negative words such as "awful", "sucks", and "unlawful" were assigned scores to calculate a Net Score per entry, which was then used to classify each post as Positive, Negative, or Neutral along with a Confidence percentage. The final results revealed that out of 14 entries, 7 were classified as Negative, 6 as Neutral, and only 1 as Positive, with an average confidence score of approximately 73%, reflecting the mixed and largely critical tone found across gaming complaints, political commentary, and tech frustration posts in the dataset. The workbook is structured across three sheets — the main Data sheet containing raw text, cleaned text, scores, and sentiment labels, a Keywords sheet listing all positive and negative trigger words used in the scoring model, and a Summary sheet with a pivot-style breakdown of sentiment counts and averages — making the entire analysis transparent, reproducible, and fully editable in Excel without any external tools or programming.
